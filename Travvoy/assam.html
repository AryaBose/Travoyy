Explore Assam: A Culinary
and Cultural Journey

  • Khorikaa
    Guwahati

    Immerse yourself in the authentic flavors of Assamese cuisine at Khorikaa. Located in the bustling city of Guwahati, this culinary gem is renowned for its mouth-watering assortment of grilled meats and fish, infused with traditional spices and herbs. Be sure to savor their signature dish - Pork with bamboo shoot, for a true taste of Assam.

  • Maihang
    Guwahati

    For a modern twist on traditional Assamese dishes, head to Maihang in Guwahati. Indulge in their elaborate Thali, featuring a delightful array of rice, lentils, vegetables, and fish curries, served with utmost care and authenticity.

  • Parampara Paradise
    Jorhat

    Experience the true essence of Assamese hospitality at Parampara Paradise in Jorhat. Delight your palate with their traditional Assamese thali, served in classic brass utensils, offering a feast for both the eyes and taste buds.

  • Gam's Delicacy
    Tezpur

    Treat yourself to the delectable sweets and snacks of Assam at Gam's Delicacy in Tezpur. From traditional delicacies like Pitha and Narikol to the tangy flavors of Tenga Doi (sour yogurt), this culinary haven promises a gastronomic delight.

  • Heritage Khorika
    Majuli

    Journey to Majuli, the world's largest river island, and dine at Heritage Khorika for an unforgettable culinary experience. Feast on fresh river fish dishes amidst serene surroundings, capturing the essence of Assam's cultural and natural heritage.

Top 5 Art Works
in Assam

  • Assamese
    Traditional Weaving

    Immerse yourself in the timeless art of Assamese traditional weaving, renowned for its intricate patterns and luxurious fabrics. Journey to weaving hubs like Sualkuchi, hailed as the "Manchester of the East," and witness master artisans meticulously crafting Muga silk, Eri silk, and Assam silk, showcasing centuries-old weaving techniques passed down through generations.

  • Bihu Dance

    Experience the pulsating rhythms and vibrant energy of Bihu dance, a quintessential cultural expression of Assam. Celebrated during the Bihu festivals, this dynamic folk dance encapsulates the essence of Assamese agrarian life, symbolizing joy, fertility, and abundance. Join in the festivities and revel in the infectious enthusiasm of Bihu dancers adorned in traditional attire.

  • Mask Making

    Delve into the ancient art of mask making, an integral part of Assamese traditional theater and cultural rituals. Admire the craftsmanship of skilled artisans as they carve intricate masks depicting mythological characters and folklore motifs. Witness these captivating masks come to life in traditional theatrical performances like Ankiya Nat and Bhaona, adding depth and drama to Assam's cultural narrative.

  • Pottery

    Explore the rustic charm of Assam's pottery tradition, characterized by its rustic simplicity and functional elegance. Journey to pottery villages such as Sualkuchi and Hajo, where artisans deftly mold clay into exquisite earthenware using traditional techniques. Marvel at the versatility of Assamese pottery, ranging from utilitarian vessels to decorative sculptures, reflecting the region's rich artistic heritage.

  • Assamese
    Music

    Immerse yourself in the melodious strains of Assamese music, a harmonious blend of devotional, narrative, and folk traditions. Discover the soul-stirring melodies of Borgeet, the devotional hymns of Vaishnavite tradition, and the rhythmic beats of Oja Pali and Zikir. Experience the resonant sounds of traditional musical instruments like the dhol, pepa, and taal, which serve as the rhythmic heartbeat of Assamese cultural expressions.

  • River Cruise
    Brahmaputra

    Experience the majesty of the Brahmaputra River with a scenic river cruise. Traverse the gentle waters of one of the world's largest rivers and behold panoramic views of verdant landscapes, quaint villages, and exotic wildlife. Immerse yourself in cultural performances and explore idyllic riverine islands such as Majuli, the largest river island in the world.

  • Wildlife Safari
    Kaziranga National Park

    Embark on an exhilarating wildlife safari in Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its conservation of the one-horned rhinoceros. Traverse the grasslands and wetlands of this biodiverse sanctuary, encountering iconic species such as tigers, elephants, and an array of avian fauna.

  • Tea Garden Tour
    Jorhat or Dibrugarh

    Delve into Assam's rich tea heritage with a guided tour of its lush tea gardens in Jorhat or Dibrugarh. Witness the meticulous process of tea cultivation, from plucking the tender leaves to their transformation into the world-famous Assam tea. Engage your senses with tea tasting sessions, sampling the distinctive flavors of Assam's finest brews.

  • Trekking
    Himalayan Foothills

    Embark on an immersive trekking adventure amidst the Himalayan foothills of Assam. Traverse the pristine wilderness of Dibru-Saikhowa National Park or Nameri National Park, navigating through dense forests, meandering rivers, and cascading waterfalls. Encounter diverse flora and fauna while soaking in the breathtaking vistas of the Himalayan landscape.

  • Exploring Tribal Villages
    and Cultural Heritage Sites

    Immerse yourself in Assam's vibrant cultural mosaic by exploring its tribal villages and heritage sites. Visit historic landmarks such as Sivasagar, Hajo, and Tezpur, adorned with ancient temples, monuments, and architectural marvels. Engage with indigenous communities, witnessing their traditional lifestyles, folk art, and rituals, and gain profound insights into Assam's rich cultural tapestry.

    Hampta Pass Trek

    Suitable Time: June to September The Hampta Pass Trek is a mesmerizing journey through the Pir Panjal Range, offering breathtaking views of lush valleys, towering peaks, and pristine alpine lakes. With moderate difficulty levels, this trek is suitable for both novice and experienced trekkers, promising an immersive Himalayan adventure.

    Pin Parvati Pass Trek

    Suitable Time: July to September For seasoned trekkers seeking an adrenaline-pumping challenge, the Pin Parvati Pass Trek is an ultimate adventure. Traversing through remote valleys, snow-capped passes, and ancient glaciers, this trek offers unparalleled vistas of the Himalayan landscape and a chance to explore the untouched beauty of Himachal Pradesh.

  • Learn More About
    Bara Bhangal Trek

    Suitable Time: June to October The Bara Bhangal Trek takes you off the beaten path into the rugged terrain and remote villages of Himachal Pradesh. With demanding trails and stunning vistas, this trek offers a glimpse into the traditional way of life in the Himalayan hinterland, making it a unique and unforgettable experience for adventure enthusiasts.

    Indrahar Pass Trek

    Suitable Time: May to October Starting from the bustling town of McLeod Ganj, the Indrahar Pass Trek is a scenic journey through the Dhauladhar Range, offering panoramic views of lush valleys, cascading waterfalls, and snow-clad peaks. With moderate difficulty levels, this trek is perfect for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts alike.

    Sar Pass Trek

    Suitable Time: May to June, September to November The Sar Pass Trek in the Parvati Valley is renowned for its scenic beauty and adventurous trails. With pine forests, meandering rivers, and snowfields, this trek offers a perfect blend of natural splendor and adrenaline-pumping excitement, making it a must-do for trekkers seeking a memorable Himalayan experience.
