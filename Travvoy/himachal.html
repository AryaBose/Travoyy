Explore Himachal : A Culinary
and Cultural Journey

  • Johnson's Cafe
    Manali

    Located amidst the scenic hills of Manali, Johnson's Cafe offers a cozy retreat and a diverse menu featuring authentic Himachali specialties. From Siddu to Dham, every dish is a culinary delight that reflects the flavors of the region, making it a must-visit for food enthusiasts.

  • Himachali Rasoi
    Shimla

    Situated in the heart of Shimla, Himachali Rasoi is a culinary haven known for its authentic Himachali cuisine. With dishes like Chana Madra and Tudkiya Bhath, prepared using locally sourced ingredients, this eatery provides a true taste of Himachal's culinary heritage.

  • Sher-e-Punjab
    Dharamshala

    Indulge in a fusion of Himachali and Punjabi flavors at Sher-e-Punjab in Dharamshala. From Kangri Dham to Sepu Vadi, the menu offers a tantalizing array of dishes amidst stunning views of the Dhauladhar Range, creating a memorable dining experience.

  • The Llama's Kitchen
    McLeod Ganj

    Experience the multicultural flavors of McLeod Ganj at The Llama's Kitchen, where Tibetan and Himachali cuisines come together harmoniously. With specialties like momos and thukpa, this eatery offers a culinary journey that reflects the diverse cultural influences of the region.

  • Nicky's Italian Kitchen
    Kasol

    For a taste of something different, head to Nicky's Italian Kitchen in Kasol for mouthwatering wood-fired pizzas and Mediterranean-inspired dishes. Set against the backdrop of the Parvati Valley, this eatery provides a refreshing culinary experience amidst the natural beauty of Himachal Pradesh.

Top 5 Festivals
in Himachal

  • Shivratri Fair

    Experience the cultural extravaganza of the Shivratri Fair in Mandi, one of Himachal's oldest and most vibrant festivals. With colorful processions, traditional folk dances, and cultural performances, the fair offers a glimpse into the spiritual and cultural heritage of the region.

  • Kullu Dussehra

    Join the festivities of Kullu Dussehra, a grand celebration marked by elaborate processions, music, and dance in the Kullu Valley. This iconic festival showcases the rich cultural traditions and religious fervor of Himachal Pradesh, making it a highlight of the state's festival calendar.

  • Losar Festival

    Celebrate the Tibetan New Year with the vibrant Losar Festival in Spiti Valley, where locals engage in ancient rituals, masked dances, and cultural performances. The festival offers a glimpse into the rich Tibetan heritage and spiritual traditions of the region.

  • Phulaich Festival

    Experience the Phulaich Festival in Kinnaur, a unique celebration that marks the end of the harvesting season. With colorful rituals, traditional attire, and folk songs, the festival provides insight into the agricultural practices and cultural traditions of the region.

  • Renuka Fair

    Immerse yourself in the cultural diversity of Himachal Pradesh at the Renuka Fair, held annually at Renuka Lake in Sirmaur district. With traditional folk dances, music, handicrafts, and local delicacies, the fair showcases the unique cultural heritage of the state, making it a must-visit for travelers.

  • Hampta Pass Trek

    The Hampta Pass Trek is a mesmerizing journey through the Pir Panjal Range, offering breathtaking views of lush valleys, towering peaks, and pristine alpine lakes. With moderate difficulty levels, this trek is suitable for both novice and experienced trekkers, promising an immersive Himalayan adventure.

  • Sar Pass Trek

    The Sar Pass Trek in the Parvati Valley is renowned for its scenic beauty and adventurous trails. With pine forests, meandering rivers, and snowfields, this trek offers a perfect blend of natural splendor and adrenaline-pumping excitement, making it a must-do for trekkers seeking a memorable Himalayan experience.

  • Pin Parvati Pass Trek

    For seasoned trekkers seeking an adrenaline-pumping challenge, the Pin Parvati Pass Trek is an ultimate adventure. Traversing through remote valleys, snow-capped passes, and ancient glaciers, this trek offers unparalleled vistas of the Himalayan landscape and a chance to explore the untouched beauty of Himachal Pradesh.

  • Bara Bhangal Trek

    The Bara Bhangal Trek takes you off the beaten path into the rugged terrain and remote villages of Himachal Pradesh. With demanding trails and stunning vistas, this trek offers a glimpse into the traditional way of life in the Himalayan hinterland, making it a unique and unforgettable experience for adventure enthusiasts.

  • Indrahar Pass Trek

    Starting from the bustling town of McLeod Ganj, the Indrahar Pass Trek is a scenic journey through the Dhauladhar Range, offering panoramic views of lush valleys, cascading waterfalls, and snow-clad peaks. With moderate difficulty levels, this trek is perfect for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts alike.

Top 5 Activities
in Himachal Pradesh

  • River Rafting in
    Beas River

    Experience the thrill of white-water rafting in the Beas River, offering adrenaline-pumping fun amidst stunning mountain scenery. With various rapids suitable for all skill levels, this activity promises an exhilarating adventure for adventure enthusiasts and thrill-seekers.

  • Paragliding in
    Bir-Billing

    Soar high above the picturesque landscapes of Bir-Billing, known as one of the best paragliding destinations in the world. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced paraglider, this activity offers an unmatched adrenaline rush and panoramic views of the Himalayas.

  • Explore Shimla's Heritage

    Discover the colonial charm and heritage of Shimla, the erstwhile summer capital of British India. From historic landmarks like Christ Church and Viceregal Lodge to scenic viewpoints like Jakhu Hill, Shimla offers a wealth of cultural and scenic attractions for travelers to explore and enjoy.

  • Camping in
    Spiti Valley

    Immerse yourself in the serene beauty of Spiti Valley with a camping experience amidst its rugged landscapes and pristine natural surroundings. Spend a night under the starlit sky, surrounded by towering peaks, ancient monasteries, and glistening rivers, offering a truly unforgettable outdoor adventure.

  • Skiing in
    Solang Valley

    Embrace the winter wonderland of Solang Valley with a thrilling skiing adventure on its snow-covered slopes. Whether you're a novice or an expert skier, the valley offers a range of slopes and facilities for an unforgettable skiing experience amidst stunning Himalayan vistas.
